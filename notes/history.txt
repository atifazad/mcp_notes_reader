The rise and fall of empires is a recurring theme in world history. The Roman Empire, at its height, controlled vast territories across Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, setting a standard for imperial governance, military organization, and cultural integration.

However, internal decay, economic troubles, and external pressures eventually led to its collapse in the West by 476 CE. Historians continue to debate whether Rome fell due to moral decline, overextension, or a combination of many factors.
